Lesson 5

Summary

This lesson shows us how packets work and makes us send messages on an unreliable network, showing us how sometimes the internet may have issues such as sending things out of order or not sending things at all.

Check for Understanding

Terminology Matching
Term Definition
Internet Protocol (IP) Rules for addressing and sending data across the internet by assigning unique numbers to each connected device
Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) Takes inventory of all packets in the datastream to ensure they are successfully sent and received.
Packet Takes inventory of all packets in the datastream to ensure they are successfully sent and received.
Datastream The way in which information travels on the Internet, not as a single piece but in chunks.
User Datagram Protoocol (UDP) Sends all packets without checking whether they were received or ordered properly.
