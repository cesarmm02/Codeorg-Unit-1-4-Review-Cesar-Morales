Lesson 6

Summary

This lesson shows us how we can use conditionals in "if", "if-else", and "if-else-if" statements to produce a certain result

Check for Understanding

Using if-else-if

When creating an if-else-if statement you should always make your first condition the most specific. Write a short paragraph responding to the questions below.
What does it mean to put the most specific case first?
Why is it important to put the most specific case first? What types of errors does it help avoid?

Answer
The most specific case first means to put the case that would be the most difficult for the user to achieve. In the lemon squeeze game, the size 15 was first because it was the highest amount so it wouldn't activate early. It's important to put the most specific case first because otherwise if the first boolean is true, it will say true even if another boolean after it is also true. In the temperature example, 86 was a high temperature, and you would've wanted it to say "its hot", but since 86 is already higher than 60, it would say "it's cool", even if the other expressions were true, because 60 was first.
